FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about EPAT shockwave therapy and our services in Long Island City, Queens, NY.

What is EPAT Shockwave Therapy and how does it work?

EPAT (Extracorporeal Pulse Activation Therapy) is a non-invasive treatment that uses targeted acoustic waves to stimulate healing in damaged tissues. The waves penetrate deep into the affected area, stimulating blood circulation, accelerating the body's natural repair processes and promoting the regeneration of damaged tendons and soft tissue. It is a safe, drug-free, surgery-free treatment with clinically proven results for chronic musculoskeletal pain.

What conditions does EPAT shockwave therapy treat?

EPAT is particularly effective for chronic tendonitis, plantar fasciitis, tennis elbow, calcific rotator cuff tendinopathy, Achilles tendinopathy, patellar tendinopathy, shin splints and other soft tissue injuries. It is beneficial for both athletes dealing with sports injuries and everyday patients experiencing chronic musculoskeletal pain. Our practitioners assess each patient individually to determine if EPAT is the right treatment for their specific condition.

Is EPAT therapy safe? Are there side effects?

Yes, EPAT is a very safe, clinically validated, non-invasive treatment. It is drug-free and surgery-free, which makes it an excellent alternative to more invasive treatments. Most patients experience little to no side effects. Some patients may experience mild temporary soreness or redness in the treated area immediately after treatment, which typically resolves quickly. Our practitioners will discuss any potential considerations specific to your condition before beginning treatment.

How many EPAT sessions will I need?

Our expert practitioners personalize treatment protocols for each individual patient. Most patients begin to notice significant improvement within 3 to 5 sessions. The total number of sessions required depends on the specific condition being treated, its severity and how long it has been present. Chronic conditions may require more sessions than acute injuries. We will give you a clear treatment plan and timeline during your initial assessment at our Long Island City clinic.

Who is a good candidate for EPAT shockwave therapy?

EPAT is ideal for patients who have chronic tendonitis or soft tissue injuries that have not responded well to conventional treatments such as rest, physical therapy or anti-inflammatory medication. It is an excellent option for those who want to avoid surgery or lengthy rehabilitation. Athletes seeking faster recovery and everyday patients dealing with long-term pain are both excellent candidates. Call us at 917-628-2144 to schedule a consultation and determine if EPAT is right for you.

Tendonitis Shockwave Therapy

Tendonitis is one of the most common and frustrating musculoskeletal conditions, often causing chronic pain that limits daily activity for months or even years. Our EPAT shockwave therapy targets the damaged tendon tissue directly with precision acoustic waves, stimulating blood circulation, breaking down scar tissue, and activating the body's natural healing response. The result is significant reduction in pain, improved flexibility and faster recovery — often without the need for surgery, drugs or lengthy downtime. We treat Achilles tendonitis, patellar tendonitis, rotator cuff tendinopathy, hamstring tendinopathy and all forms of chronic tendon pain for patients throughout Long Island City, Queens and all of New York.

Plantar Fasciitis Treatment

Plantar fasciitis — chronic heel pain caused by inflammation of the plantar fascia — is one of the most common conditions we treat with EPAT shockwave therapy. For patients who have tried rest, orthotics, stretching and physical therapy without adequate relief, EPAT offers a clinically proven solution that targets the root cause of the inflammation. Our acoustic wave treatment stimulates tissue repair and significantly reduces pain in the heel and arch, restoring comfortable walking and standing for patients across Long Island City and Queens. Service 03

Tennis Elbow & Rotator Cuff

Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis) and calcific rotator cuff tendinopathy are two of the most painful and persistent soft tissue conditions affecting the upper extremities. Both conditions respond exceptionally well to EPAT shockwave therapy. For tennis elbow, our treatment reduces the inflammation and breakdown in the extensor tendons of the forearm, restoring grip strength and eliminating elbow pain. For rotator cuff conditions, including calcific tendinopathy where calcium deposits have formed in the tendon, EPAT has been shown to break down calcifications and stimulate healing of the surrounding tissue. We treat patients from all walks of life — from professional athletes to office workers — throughout Long Island City and the greater Queens area.
